1. Background Information

R25e finished 7th out of 86 teams at the FSAE Michigan competition, a stellar result that tied with the teamâ€™s all-time best performance. Yet, it shared much of its suspension mechanical design with the previous car, R24e. This is due to the short development cycle, as well as designs becoming increasingly matured. Small incremental changes are safe, but stagnation is also a possibility.

I intend to overhaul the component designs for this season, trading potential testing time for the opportunity to implement different ideas that push past the bottlenecks. Many of the ideas have been thrown around in suspension departments even with the previous cohorts, but they were not considered seriously. Given the time , my new designs will not drastically differ from that of my seniors, but I hope that experiences gained in trial and error can provide valuable insights for future generations of suspension designers.
